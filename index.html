On collaboration
100xbtr is a collaborative practice, not a hierarchy. Titles and reporting lines come later, if at all. What matters first is that each person's contribution is understood, valued, and protected — and that decisions get made without gridlock.
Roles and domains are open questions to be worked out together. What's listed below reflects what each person brings — not what they're confined to.

Roadmap to exit
The four phases
Phase 1
Months 1–6
Build the proof
Design and build 2–3 reference pairs. Develop brand identity and founding story. Spend $3–5K on components. Target: 1 paying customer + press-ready photography.
Phase 2
Months 6–18
Go to market
Sell via AudiogoN, US Audio Mart, Instagram. Target interior designers and custom home builders in LA. Goal: 8–15 pairs at $2–5K = $16–75K revenue.
Phase 3
Years 2–4
Build the brand asset
Expand to 2–3 product lines. Get reviewed by Stereophile or The Absolute Sound. Partner with AV integrators. Target: $250K–$500K/yr revenue.
Phase 4
Years 5–10
Exit
Documented sales, press coverage, strong margins, proprietary design language = acquirable. Likely buyers: McIntosh Group, Sound United, or private equity.
